Recurring Limitations — What Researchers Could NOT Solve
🔴 Overlapping & Touching Cells
Standard NMS discards valid overlapping boxes. Models consistently miss cells that are clumped. Even recent 2025 papers acknowledge this causes systematic under-counting in dense smears.
Admitted by: CFPNet-M, YOLOv4, PlasmoCount, systematic review 2024
🔴 Incomplete / Sparse Annotations
Datasets only annotate infected cells or a subset of RBCs. End-to-end detectors (Faster R-CNN, YOLO) treat unannotated cells as background, suppressing detections. No paper has cleanly solved this.
Admitted by: COCO-format NIH dataset paper 2025, semi-supervised survey
🟡 Extreme Class Imbalance
RBCs dominate (96%+ of cells). Rare stages like schizont and gametocyte have <200 examples. Data augmentation helps but doesn't fully close the gap. Focal loss adoption is rare in this domain.
Admitted by: YOLOv4 paper, systematic review, clinical deployment paper
🟡 Stain & Domain Variation
Giemsa staining varies by lab, operator, and slide age. Models trained on one site fail on another. Color jitter augmentation partially helps but cross-site generalization remains an open problem.
Admitted by: Continual learning paper 2025, clinical settings paper 2023
🔴 No Clinical Explainability
Most systems output a label ("infected") with no spatial explanation of where the parasite is inside the cell. Doctors cannot trust or verify it. Grad-CAM exists in the literature but is not integrated into end-to-end detection pipelines.
Admitted by: Trustworthy DL paper, XAI survey, clinical adoption paper
🔴 Thick Smear Neglect
WHO recommends thick smears for clinical diagnosis. Virtually all published work uses thin smears only. Thick smears have denser, degraded cell morphology that breaks existing pipelines entirely.
Admitted by: Systematic review 2024, WHO guidelines, multiple survey papers

🎯 Where MalariAI Fits — The Three Novelty Claims
N1
Label-Resilient Segmentation
Universal cell detection via watershed — finds every cell regardless of annotation completeness. Directly solves the incomplete-annotation problem no paper has cleanly addressed.
N2
Density-Aware Overlap Handling
Distance-transform watershed separates touching cells that NMS-based detectors routinely delete. Provably better recall in dense smear regions.
N3
End-to-End XAI Integration
Grad-CAM++ output on classified crops gives clinicians a spatial heatmap of the parasite. First paper to integrate this into a full detection + classification + explanation pipeline.
